Polynomial rings are Vector Spaces


Fact: Polynomial rings are infinite dimensional vector spaces over $k$.

Example

\begin{align*} R = k&\hspace{-3mm}[x,y]\\ = k & \oplus k \langle x,y \rangle \oplus k \langle x^2, xy, y^2 \rangle \oplus\\ & \oplus k \langle x^3, x^2y, xy^2, y^3 \rangle \oplus \cdots \end{align*}
