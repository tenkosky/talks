Combinatorial Solution

Laman's Theorem: A drawing of $G$ is rigid (in the plane) if and only if there a subest of edges $F\subseteq E$ such that

1) $|F|=2|V|-3$

Needs enough edges (relatively small)

2) $|F'|\leq 2|V(F')|-3$ for all $F'\subseteq F$

Edges can't be in a clump


Example: Cargo Bridge
